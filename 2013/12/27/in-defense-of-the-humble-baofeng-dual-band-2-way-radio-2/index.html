In Defense of the Humble Baofeng Dual Band 2-Way Radio

12-27-2013
#firearms
Firearms

The future is never certain, but some things can assumed. Any major conflict which includes large portions of the citizenry will be a come as you are affair. There will be all level of skills and equipment utilized, and communication gear will be no exception. There will be Family Radio (FRS/GRMS), Ham Radio, Marine radios, Aviation Band and MURS (Business radios) equipment for local tactical communications. Here is the punch line, “The Dual Band Baofeng Radio” can talk to all of them. WRSA asked, that are our thoughts about Baofeng’s UV-5RA, here is my reply. It also receives Commercial FM and NOAA weather reports. I have played with the BF-888s ($16), UV-3R+ ($30), UV-5R ($30) and the UV-B6 ($40) and each has their own merits. The BF-888 is cheap, has 16 channels that must be set with a computer cable and talks on and around 70cm (including FRS/GRMS), it uses a simple rotary switch with no display and is dead nuts simple, even your well meaning idiot neighbor with the shotgun full of bird shot can run this radio. The UV-3R+ has no alpha tags which is kind of annoying, and runs only about 2 watts, which is more then enough for inter-squad communications, but the smaller battery with equally short talk time, prevents this from being my go to radio, when for the same price you can have the more capable UV-5R. The UV-5R is the sweet spot, with 6 character alpha display, extended batteries, battery boxes for AAs and a battery eliminator that has a cigarette lighter end to use in your car. The lack of accessories and the 5 character display, which is a pain for Ham use in the US, since many repeaters have 6 character identifiers make is kind of a non-starter. It has a more defined feel in the hand, and the second knob (in place of the led light, the Chinese love to put on these things) is nice for rolling though the pre-sets, but all in all, it gives up some capabilities and is not worth that additional $10. The stock antennas are not horrible and more then sufficient for it’s intended purpose, but a Nagoya NA-701 ($10) will do a pretty nice job, not as short as the stock unit, but with better gain, antennas seems to be that way, big tends to be better. There is nothing magical about the radios, but they are reasonably well built (but not water proof, or resistant for that matter) and very open in terms frequencies they can cover and cheap. Inter-Squad communications is about coordinating when a building gets breached, or calling a medic, it’s short, not strategic and of little intelligence worth. Assume that you are heard and can be triangulated in on, when you use these, so always use the least amount of power as possible. This can be accomplished by setting the power setting to low, and further reduced by using a stubby antenna with negative gain. * these radios can not be used on all these frequencies legally, get your Ham ticket use what is legal now. In some terrible future, use your little Chinese radio to talk to others no matter what they bring, even the lowly FRS/GRMS which was used to good effect for squad level communications in Iraq, until the military replaced it with a more expensive tax-payer paid for radio.
