Minimal IFAK

01-17-2014
#firearms
Firearms

We carry too much “stuff” and too much of it we need easy access to. This seems like the minimal IFAK that would treat the single injury that you will treat in the Causality Care Under Fire stage of medical intervention, hemorrhaging. This will either be self or buddy aid to prevent exsanguination. We have a lot of items competing for space on our belts and this kit is only 4.5”H x 6.5”L x 2”D. Other stuff could be carried elsewhere, or a team could have a squad medic bag, if the team is trying to optimize load or cost. I know the idea is what is carried on you, is used on you, like they do it in the military, but we might have to think outside the box. We are not backed up with tax payer funding nor able to maneuver with the same load as 18 year old recruits so increasing the sharing of equipment might be indicated. There is a trap in saying we need to do it the “real” way, just like Washington trying to fight a European style war. This is not to say that we should not learn from .Mil, just that it might not be the only way. Here is what I see in this picture

  • Condor Outdoor CRYE Multicam First Response Pouch (Non-Multicam can be found cheaper)
  • Combat Gauze
  • Tourniquet
  • 4 Inch Israeli bandage
  • Gloves (which I would replace with a small roll of duck tape)
  • I would squeeze some more regular Gauze in, to either reenforce the Combat Gauze or wipe out the wound bowl prior to it’s use)
