Can the “War Baby” protect your Baby?

Using the M1 Carbine for Home Defense.

What do you need in a home defense firearm? Light and maneuverable, easy of use, powerful enough to quickly incapacitate an intruder intent on harming you and you loved ones and enough capacity to reduce the need for reloading. A place to mount a light, a method for quick acquisition and muzzle blast that doesn’t leave you deaf and blind. Shotguns are often a popular choice, but the limited ammunition supply makes them more difficult to run and any shot near or with loved ones in the background more dangerous. Recoil is a factor and at the typical across the room distances aim is still required. Pistols, of course, are the easiest to maneuver, light and fast, and only the weakness of the pistol round and the most difficult to hit with under stress, with their shorter sight radius and no shoulder contact for support. AR-15s are a popular and logical choice; powerful, infinitely customizable, very ergonomic and light. Mounting a red dot type optic on them is easy and makes them very fast; mounting a white light is equally trivial and necessary for target identification. The .223 round which is the most common round fired from a AR-15 stops quickly when it hits contraction materials like wood and drywall reducing risk to family and neighbors. The biggest down side is the amount of noise and muzzle flash created by in a typical 16-inch barrel. Low flash powders in some rounds can help with flash, but there is no way to reduce the decibels without the difficulty of a suppressor and the associated weapon length increase The M1 Carbine is under estimated as a Home Defense firearm. It was actually designed during World War 2 to provide an easier to handle firearm than the 1911 pistol that may non-infantry roles in the military was using for a personal defense weapon. 6 ½ Million of these were build. It’s a 5.2 pound, 3 foot long, gas operated, semi-automatic rifle that generates 967 foot pounds of energy firing 110gr round nose bullet 1,990 feet per second, which is equivalent to a .357 magnum fired from the same length (16”) barrel. This was a near perfect self defense weapon in 1941, but it can use some improvements to bring it into the 21 century. Red Dot sight and a white light are no brainers, required for fast acquisition and target identification. Handling is somewhat enhanced with a pistol grip, but not absolutely required. Lastly a folding stock makes it more compact, and if that meets a particular storage need, go for it. It might not be as cool as a tricked out AR-15 (which is most ways is a better weapon), but your not exactly unarmed with the M1 Carbine. The manual of arms is simple, works well for small people and is much easier to shot well then a pistol which was it’s primary purpose in World War 2 (replacing the side arm that is). Soft p or some of the fancy all copper hollow point ammunition will greatly enhance it’s effectiveness, and muzzle flash and noise are greatly reduced by shooting what is essentially a pistol round through a 16 inch tube.
