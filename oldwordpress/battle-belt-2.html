I’ve put together a War Belt (Battle Belt). I have not really run it hard and even if it works well, I’m not sure of its utility. There is the full LBE, with backpack for remote operations and if you are in the field a long time carrying food, shelter and significant ordnance , and the active shooter bag, of which I run the US Peacekeeper Rapid Deployment Bag and is great for the “grab and go” minuteman or the classic bump in the night. A belt is a poor choice for vehicle work, were a chest rig is more accessible and easier on the back. So, what niche does it fill for the armed citizen? It’s much easier on the back then the shoulder bag, and substantially less weight then a full LBE kit. If I was working security in the neighborhood where food and water are not an issue, even though the belt could be combined with a small camelback with water and some calories. Even with a butt stock pouch with a magazine, 150 rounds does not allow for the endless exchange of projectiles, but it’s vastly better then a sharp stick. It also is faster then the bag, things are where you expect them with no shifting like a bag. There is a reason all the cool 3-gun kids run a belt. If speed is the name of the game I think the belt is king. The only 2 advantages I can see to having some extra pounds around the middle, is you aren’t going to be the first one to starve and you get to carry a little extra equipment on a belt, and since there is a gear queer in me trying to get out, I have some stuff on the belt I most likely don’t need, but I’ll go though what I have and what might go with a little experience and experimentation.
