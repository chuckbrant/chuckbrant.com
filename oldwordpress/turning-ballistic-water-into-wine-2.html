I have a neighbor, a good guy but not the brightest bulb on the proverbial Christmas tree. He honestly believes himself well armed with a 12-gauge pump gun and 500 rounds of 7 1/2 skeet loads. There may be a day that he will be hard pointing the entrance to the neighborhood, but will need a ballistic upgrade. So one approach and the most expedient is to create a cut shell. But a better way, which is more consistent in terms of accuracy and shell durability is to recast the shot into a slug. (yes that is the back of a chess clock in the picture) Lee makes an inexpensive 1 ounce mold that is used to cast a simple slug. If you are really desperate you can use the correct size wrench socket as your mold with slightly inferior results. Using a utility knife you trim off the star crimp, drop in a newly cast slug and seal it back up. You can simply melt some crayon or use a hot glue gun. The slightly more sophisticated approach is to put on an over shot card and re-crimp the shell case. Over shot cards are just a plastic or paper disk, and putting the roll crimp back on just takes a second or two on your drill press. Both the cards and the tool to create the crimp are available at Ballistic Products. You, of course, can cut your own overshot cards, but that might be a clue you just have too much time on your hands Turning $25 worth of field shot into 100 rounds of slug is worth the effort and might be a skill that can help that well intentioned neighbor, help provide more security and cost you literally nothing.
