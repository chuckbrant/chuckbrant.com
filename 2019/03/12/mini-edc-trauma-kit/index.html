Mini EDC Trauma Kit

03-12-2019
All Posts

The goal is to build a trauma kit that can be carried in a back pocket of any pants. Similar in size to a wallet, but with live saving gear. Know that something this small will be a compromise, and assumes that professional care is but a couple of minutes away and 911 service is available. If you are going to a remote location, or emergency care will take considerable time to get you will need to have more capabilities with you.

TC3 Document by Robert M. Miller[/caption] In combat these are the 3 most preventable causes of death are losing too much blood, letting air into the chest cavity, or having a compromised airway. These are the things we need to address the best we can with what little gear we are going to be to carry everyday (EDC) in our normal day to day lives. We are not Batman nor a combat soldier, yet bad things happen to good people and active shooters for exmaple are a real thing. I can have more gear in my car, my house, at the range and in the office but sometimes you just have what’s in your pocket.

Min-E-Med Kit[/caption] First you need a pouch, and the Min-E-Med Kit is a nice pouch with a lift tab and elastic to hold the contents securely. It comes in a few colors and and is very flat. The manufacturer is Immediate Casualty Care and states the kit is designed to store the following: (1) Quikclot Combat Gauze, (1) SWAT-T, (2) Frog Tape or other flat tape. The SWAT-T tourniquet is basically a long wide strip of rubber. It can be difficult to put it on with one hand (biting the end helps), and is not as secure as other tourniquet dure to the closure is just tucking in the end. But remember we said that size will cause some compromises. I has the advantage of being flat and small and can be used as a pressure bandage to secure wound packing. It is size independent meaning it can be used on pets and children. If you haven’t seen SkinnyMedic on Youtube he is worth checking out. Here is a video SkinnyMedic going over the SWAT-T Tourniquet https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-wr9uwj2-i0

SkinnyMedic on the SWAT-T

The next piece of gear we need to be concerned about is wound packing. Bandages are not about soaking up the blood but are about getting pressure on blood vessels that are in locations were a tourniquet is not usable, or the level of bleeding is severe enough to warrant a tourniquet. We do this packing the wound (not a chest would, we will get to that later) with gauze, this generates pressure and gives a medium for our natural clotting abilities. If money is a major concern, than plain gauze is the answer. If you can go down the middle of the road on price, you can add a 3 inch by 2 foot strip for about $10 (when purchasing 2) plus , or you can go with a full 4 foot piece of combat gauze if money is no object. This “Combat Gauze” has a homeostatic agent, which makes it more effective. I would not go with loose agent like the 1st generation stuff, as windy day can make it pretty unpleasant if you get it your eyes. But regardless of which you get, remember to “pack it to the bone”, as it’s purpose it to generate enough pressure to help close of blood vessels. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8zNbMLWeaoI

The H & H Mini Compression Bandage is the smallest compression bandage I could find, with the NAR 4” Flat ETD running a close second. Neither has a compression bar like the Israeli or other larger banadges, using just elastic for tension. They have 4x6 pads, with about 42 inches of unstrected elastic band and velco and the ends to keep them in place. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T6L4S4y6r9A
